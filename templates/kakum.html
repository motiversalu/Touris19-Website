Kakum National Park

Kakum National Park, in Ghana's Central Region, is 375 square kilometers in size and is located along the coast. It was established as a reserve in 1931 and only became a national park in 1992. It is one of only three places in Africa with a 350-meter-long canopy bridge that connects seven tree tops. Diana monkey, big bongo antelope, yellow-backed duiker, and African elephant are among the park's most threatened animals. Bird Life International has designated it as an Important Bird Area, with the bird area fully encompassing the park area. Wikipedia
